# Investor FAQ

## 1. Is this just another translation app?

No. Translation is only the first step. The product turns real-world English into a structured beginner lesson: sentence breakdown, vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation and saved review.

---

## 2. Why focus on Vietnamese learners?

The first wedge is narrow and clear: Vietnamese beginners need explanations in Vietnamese, simple examples and less academic grammar language. This focus makes the UX and AI prompt much sharper than a generic language-learning product.

---

## 3. Why not just use ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is powerful but generic. This app provides a dedicated mobile workflow:

```text
scan/upload/paste → OCR review → structured lesson → save → review
```

It also validates AI output before rendering so the UI is reliable.

---

## 4. What is the initial MVP?

Phase 0:

- camera/gallery/paste input
- OCR
- OCR review/edit
- AI lesson generation
- translation/sentence/vocabulary/grammar/pronunciation
- save/history
- loading/error/empty states
- privacy/cost guard

---

## 5. What makes the product defensible?

Early defensibility is product focus, not model ownership:

- Vietnamese beginner-first teaching style
- structured lesson schema
- scan-to-learn workflow
- saved review loop
- AI quality gates
- cost-controlled BYOK/paid managed model

Longer-term defensibility can come from user learning history, review data, content patterns and retention features.

---

## 6. How does the business avoid high AI cost?

The app uses several controls:

- max text length
- quota/rate limit
- no AI call when opening saved lesson
- retry max 1
- BYOK for free/power users
- paid managed AI with quota

---

## 7. Will free users need an API key?

Not to open the app. But for real AI generation, free users can either use a small trial quota or bring their own API key. Paid users do not need any token.

---

## 8. What are the main risks?

Main risks:

- AI output quality
- OCR accuracy
- AI cost at scale
- user retention after first translation
- privacy/trust
- competition from large platforms

The current roadmap addresses these through schema validation, OCR review, local saved lessons, cost guardrails and closed beta testing.

---

## 9. What traction should be proven first?

Before scaling, prove:

- users generate lessons from real content
- explanations are perceived as useful
- users save lessons
- users return to review
- AI/OCR errors are manageable
- cost per generated lesson is acceptable
- some users show willingness to pay for managed AI

---

## 10. What would funding be used for?

Priority use:

- finish Phase 0 MVP
- improve AI/OCR quality
- run closed beta
- measure retention and paid intent
- prepare store/privacy/release readiness
- run small GTM experiments
